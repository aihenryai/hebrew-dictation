The original research that led here:
Today's mic capture uses `cpal` (cross-platform). To also capture the OTHER side of a Zoom/Meet call (what's playing through the speakers, not just the mic), Windows needs **WASAPI loopback capture** вҖ” a distinct API mode, not just "another cpal input device." `cpal`'s own WASAPI backend is not confirmed to expose loopback directly; the dedicated [`wasapi`](https://docs.rs/wasapi) crate does, with a documented loopback capture example (simultaneous capture + render on separate threads) вҖ” that's the concrete starting point, verify at implementation time whether newer `cpal` has closed this gap. Real design questions Henry needs to weigh in on before implementation starts, not just engineering: (a) mix mic + loopback into one stream, or keep them as two channels/two transcripts merged after the fact (affects diarization quality вҖ” mixing loses the "which side of the call" signal for free, that a separate-channels approach would preserve); (b) new permission/UX flow (Windows will very likely surface its own "app is capturing audio" indicator, similar to screen-recording prompts) вҖ” needs product decisions, not just code. Recommend brainstorming this properly (spec+plan, like SRT export got) rather than jumping straight to implementation, given the open design questions.
